PANOR: Patient-Augmented Networked Omni-Records: A Generative AI-Powered Clinical Intelligence System
Submission Details
Hackathon Category: Healthcare
Cohort: HEC Generative AI Training Cohort 3, Aspire Pakistan
Development Team:  Noorullah, Habiba, Aroosa, Sebighatullah
1. The Problem: A Fragmented Landscape
The primary challenge in Pakistan's healthcare system is structural fragmentation. Consider a standard scenario: a patient visits multiple specialists across different districts in a single month. In the current infrastructure, each clinic operates as an "island" of information.

Cross-Prescriber Blindness: Doctors are often unaware of medications prescribed by other practitioners, leading to dangerous drug-drug interactions.
Contextual Information Gaps: Lab technicians often receive orders without the clinical rationale, preventing them from prioritizing critical results or flagging anomalies effectively.
Time Constraints: Physicians managing high patient volumes (60–100 patients daily) have roughly 3–4 minutes per consultation. They cannot manually aggregate data from paper files, WhatsApp messages, and patient memory in this window.

PANOR is engineered to eliminate these information silos by providing a unified, AI-enhanced clinical record.
2. The Solution: Clinical Intelligence at Scale
PANOR is a web-based platform that replaces traditional, passive record-keeping with active intelligence. At its core is the Clinical Record Thread, a lifelong, patient-owned history anchored to a unique Medical ID.

The platform utilizes three specialized interfaces:

Patient Interface: Simplifies intake and symptom reporting.
Doctor Interface: Provides a real-time clinical co-pilot for decision support.
Lab Technician Interface: Translates clinical intent into technical action.
3. How Generative AI Powers PANOR
The AI within PANOR is not a secondary assistant but a core engine embedded in the clinical workflow.
Clinical Intake & Summarization
Patients can describe symptoms in Urdu or English via voice or text. The AI processes this natural language to produce a structured clinical summary, identifying chief complaints, duration, and severity before the doctor even begins the consultation.
Real-Time Clinical Co-Pilot
As a physician enters notes, the AI acts as a parallel reasoning engine. It surfaces differential diagnoses and urgency alerts based on the context of the current visit and the patient’s historical data (e.g., flagging cardiac risks for a diabetic patient presenting with chest discomfort).
Drug Safety Guardian
The system performs a structural check against the patient’s entire medication history across all providers. If a new prescription poses a risk when combined with existing medications, the AI blocks the issuance and suggests a safer alternative.
Lab Instruction Translation
AI bridges the gap between clinical intent and laboratory execution. It converts notes like "rule out bacterial co-infection" into structured instructions for the technician, including specific tests (CBC, CRP) and a priority flag.
Epidemiology Radar
By aggregating anonymized data, PANOR detects localized disease clusters. This provides an early warning system for outbreaks, such as Dengue or Cholera, before they reach a crisis point.
4. Technical Architecture
PANOR is built on a modern, high-performance tech stack designed for real-time responsiveness.

Frontend: React-based web application featuring three role-specific dashboards.
Backend: Node.js with a FastAPI service layer for AI orchestration.
AI Engine: Claude API (model: claude-sonnet-4-20250514) with streaming enabled.
Voice Integration: Web Speech API for multilingual (Urdu/English) support.
Data Structure: Patient records are maintained as structured JSON objects for the MVP, ensuring append-only integrity for clinical events.

6. Real-World Impact
In the short term, PANOR reduces medication errors and diagnostic cycles. Its automated follow-up loop (48 hours post-visit) ensures patient compliance and early detection of deterioration. In the long term, it serves as the foundational infrastructure for a national digital health system where a portable Medical ID ensures continuity of care regardless of geography.
7. Ethical Foundations
PANOR is designed with a "Human-in-the-Loop" philosophy:

Support, Not Authority: Every AI output is labeled as a suggestion; the physician remains the final decision-maker.
Privacy by Design: The Epidemiology Radar uses exclusively anonymized data.
Patient Agency: Records are consent-gated and fully exportable or deletable upon request.

9. Closing Statement
The failure of healthcare is often a failure of information movement. PANOR solves this by applying generative AI directly to the clinical workflow. By ensuring the right information reaches the right person at the right time, we can transform the healthcare experience for patients, doctors, and technicians alike.
